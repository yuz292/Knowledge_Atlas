Potential Confounds

A confound occurs when two claims appear to support each other but may both be caused by a shared unmeasured variable. In architectural neuroscience, common confounds include occupant self-selection bias, building quality co-variation, measurement overlap between stimuli, and population heterogeneity.

Note: Full confound detection requires graph path analysis. This view shows structural proxies based on theory co-occurrence in debate clusters.
