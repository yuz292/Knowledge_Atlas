# T1.g — Final report + reflection

**Track**: T1 (Image Tagging)
**Span**: Weeks 7–8
**Hardness**: Medium
**Points**: 10
**Verification**: AI primary grade + instructor reads every final report (per AI_GRADING_DESIGN § 11 Phase 3)

---

## What the student must produce

A 2,000–3,000-word final report in `160sp/tracks/t1/{student_id}/final_report.md` structured as:

1. **Executive summary** (~ 200 words) — what was built, what the student learned, what the state of the 500-image tag set is.
2. **Method** (~ 400 words) — schema, kappa pairing, HITL adjudication, classifier approach; with one figure (confusion matrix or provenance breakdown) embedded.
3. **Results** (~ 500 words) — final kappa, final macro-F1 on held-out test, tag distribution across frameworks.
4. **Discussion** (~ 500 words) — three insights about the schema, three insights about cross-framework ambiguity, three open questions.
5. **Reflection** (~ 400 words) — what was hardest; what the student would do differently if they had another 4 weeks; what they learned about doing research in an iterative-labelling setting.
6. **References** (APA) — minimum 8 references, at least 3 from the T1 framework pages' classic-5 reference lists.

## Scoring bands

### Completeness

- **0** — < 1200 words, or missing sections.
- **1** — 1200–1799 words, all sections present but thin.
- **2** — 1800–2199 words, 6+ references.
- **3** — 2200+ words, 8+ references, at least one embedded figure.

### Quality

Exemplar-anchored per section.

- **0** — Report is essentially a stitched-together weekly log with no synthesis.
- **1** — Sections are present but discussion is surface-level.
- **2** — Discussion identifies specific, defensible insights and open questions.
- **3** — Discussion identifies specific insights **and** connects them to the T1 frameworks the student tagged against (e.g., "the prospect–refuge confusion pattern recurs at the image level because both frameworks predict the observer's attention is drawn outward from a protected position; this ambiguity is a theoretical, not a labelling, problem").

### Reflection

Standard bands with elevated threshold (specificity 5 for band 2, 8 for band 3, because this is the longest deliverable).

## Late / missing

The final report is the end of the track. Late penalties apply but extensions are available if coordinated with the track lead by Week 8 Monday.
