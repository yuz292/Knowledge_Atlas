# T1.c — Inter-rater kappa with second tagger ≥ 0.7

**Track**: T1 (Image Tagging)
**Span**: Week 4
**Hardness**: Medium-hard
**Points**: 10
**Verification**: Automated (Atlas tag DB + kappa recomputation) + AI re-grade of rubric bands
**Rubric authored**: 2026-04-17

---

## What the student must produce

1. A second student as the paired tagger (the track lead assigns pairs in Week 3).
2. **Both students independently tag the same 80-image set** drawn from the shared-batch pool.
3. **A kappa report** (markdown file) in the shared repo with: the 80 image ids, both taggers' assigned tags, Cohen's kappa computation (scipy or by hand), the list of disagreements with a short adjudication note for each, and a final resolved tag for each disagreement.
4. Target: **kappa ≥ 0.70**. Below 0.70 → band 0–1 unless the student's disagreement-adjudication demonstrates genuine engagement with schema ambiguity.

## Scoring bands (three criteria, 0–3)

### Completeness

- **0** — Missing the partner's rows, or < 50 images tagged.
- **1** — Partner's rows present but < 80 images, or kappa report missing sections.
- **2** — All spec elements present; 80 images tagged by both; kappa computed; disagreements listed.
- **3** — All band-2 conditions plus: adjudication notes address every disagreement and the resolved tags are pushed back into the atlas_tag DB.

### Quality

- **0** — Kappa < 0.40 (effectively chance agreement) or kappa computation is wrong.
- **1** — Kappa 0.40–0.59.
- **2** — Kappa 0.60–0.74; disagreements adjudicated with at least one-sentence schema-referenced explanations.
- **3** — Kappa ≥ 0.75, and at least 5 disagreements have explanations citing specific image features and specific schema categories.

### Reflection

Standard bands from `rubrics/README.md § 3`. Reflection prompt: "Which schema category is hardest for pairs of taggers to agree on, and what would you propose to fix it?"
