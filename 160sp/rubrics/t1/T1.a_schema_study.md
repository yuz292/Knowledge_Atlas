# T1.a — Tag-schema study + first 20 tagged images

**Track**: T1 (Image Tagging)
**Span**: Week 3
**Hardness**: Easy
**Points**: 5
**Verification**: Automated (Atlas tag DB query) + manual check on the schema-study doc
**Rubric authored**: 2026-04-17
**Rubric approved**: pending Week-3 planning meeting

---

## What the student must produce

1. A short schema-study note (~ 300 words) that lists the tag categories in the current schema, identifies which two or three categories are most ambiguous, and proposes one concrete disambiguation rule the student expects to need.
2. **Twenty images** tagged in the Atlas tag interface, with a minimum of: image id, one or more framework-relevant tags, a 0–1 confidence score per tag, and a brief (one-sentence) rationale per tag.

## How it is scored

Raw score 0–9 → rescaled to 5 points.

### Completeness

- **0** — No schema study, or < 10 images tagged.
- **1** — Schema study is one paragraph or names the schema without engagement; 10–14 images.
- **2** — Schema study addresses the prompt; 15–19 images.
- **3** — Schema study engages with ambiguities; 20 images, each carrying confidence + rationale.

### Quality

- **0** — Tags are mostly wrong against the schema; rationales are absent or generic.
- **1** — Tags are right on the clear cases but wrong on the hard cases; rationales do not address the ambiguity.
- **2** — Tags are correct; rationales are specific; confidence scores vary sensibly (not all 1.0).
- **3** — Tags correct, rationales specific, confidence scores well-calibrated, **and** at least two rationales cite a specific feature of the image that the schema addresses.

### Reflection

- **0** — No schema-study note, or a note with nothing substantive.
- **1** — A note that lists the schema without comment.
- **2** — A note that names one specific ambiguity and proposes one disambiguation rule.
- **3** — A note that proposes a disambiguation rule *and* predicts which of the student's 20 tags are most likely to disagree with a second tagger.

## Verification procedure (TA)

Run `verification/t1_query.sql` filtered to `tagged_by = {student_id}` and `tagged_at BETWEEN week3_start AND week3_end`. Confirm row count ≥ 20.

Open three randomly-sampled rows; check that each has the tag, confidence, and rationale populated. Compare three of the student's tags to the schema definition — the TA should agree on at least two of three for a score of 2+ on Quality.

Read the schema-study note against the Reflection band.

Estimated grading time per student: 6–8 minutes.

## Late / missing

T1.a is the track entry-point. Late submissions receive the usual − 1 per day late, floored at 0 after 3 days. A student who has not submitted T1.a by the Week-4 planning meeting should be flagged to the track lead.
