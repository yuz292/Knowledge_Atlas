# A0 — Orientation, environment, first articles

**Track**: Common (all students)
**Span**: Weeks 1–2
**Hardness**: Easy
**Points**: 5
**Verification**: Automated (unified pipeline registry query)
**Rubric authored**: 2026-04-17
**Rubric approved**: pending Week-3 planning meeting

---

## What the student must produce

1. A one-paragraph orientation note (roughly 200 words) posted to their personal section of the course shared-doc, describing their background, which of the four tracks they are choosing, and one question they have about the sprint.
2. Confirmation that their environment works end-to-end: they can read the course site, they can log in to the admin-facing portions of the site that their role grants, and they have found and opened the relevant track's hub page.
3. **Five articles** entered into the unified pipeline registry via `ka_contribute.html` with: PDF upload, DOI, citation block, abstract pasted in, and at least one evidentiary-framework tag. Articles must be on a topic relevant to the course (environment + cognition, architecture + neuroscience, one of the 11 T1 frameworks, or a T1.5 domain theory).

## How it is scored

Raw score is 0–9 (three criteria × 0–3 scale). Rescaled to 5 points by the formula in `rubrics/README.md` § 3.2.

### Completeness (0–3)

- **0** — No articles submitted, or no orientation note, or environment not set up.
- **1** — < 3 articles, or orientation note is one line, or environment partially set up (e.g. can read site but can't submit).
- **2** — All three parts present; 5 articles submitted meeting the minimum spec.
- **3** — 5 articles submitted, each with abstract and evidentiary tag, plus one or two articles clearly chosen to stretch the student's reading.

### Quality (0–3)

- **0** — Articles are off-topic (not environment-cognition related), duplicates of each other, or have broken PDFs / missing DOI.
- **1** — Articles are on-topic but the metadata is sloppy: wrong citation format, missing abstract, or no evidentiary tag.
- **2** — Articles are on-topic, metadata is clean, tags are sensible.
- **3** — Articles are on-topic, metadata is clean, tags are sensible, **and** the orientation note frames a real question (not "I want to learn more about this").

### Reflection (0–3)

- **0** — No orientation note or a note that does not engage with the sprint.
- **1** — A note that describes the student's background but does not engage with the sprint or the question.
- **2** — A note that identifies one specific question or one specific expected difficulty.
- **3** — A note that connects a specific question to a specific T1 framework or T1.5 theory the student has already begun reading about, demonstrating pre-sprint reading engagement.

## Verification procedure (TA)

Run the Track-2 automated query (see `verification/t2_query.sql`) against `pipeline_registry_unified.db` filtered to `submitted_by = {student_id}` and `submitted_at BETWEEN week1_start AND week2_end`. Confirm row count ≥ 5 and the "flagged_ok" column is TRUE for each row.

Manual check: open the student's orientation note in the shared doc; confirm it is present and scores against the three bands above.

Estimated grading time per student: 5–7 minutes.

## Late / missing policy

A0 is low-stakes. Students who miss the Week-2 Monday deadline are flagged in the grading sheet but can submit by the Week-3 Monday with a − 1 penalty on the rescaled score. After Week-3 Monday, the score stands at zero unless the student has a documented emergency.
