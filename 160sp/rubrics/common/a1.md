# A1 — Schema study, second article batch, first reflection

**Track**: Common (all students)
**Span**: Weeks 2–3
**Hardness**: Medium
**Points**: 5
**Verification**: Automated (unified pipeline registry) + manual checklist on reflection
**Rubric authored**: 2026-04-17
**Rubric approved**: pending Week-3 planning meeting

---

## What the student must produce

1. **A short schema study** (~ 400 words) posted to the course shared-doc that names one T1 framework (of the 11) and writes a one-paragraph summary of how the framework's main claim would appear in an environment-cognition setting relevant to the student's chosen track. The student should cite at least one of the 5 classic references and one of the 5 recent neuroscience references listed on the T1 framework page.
2. **Fifteen more articles** into the unified pipeline registry (total 20 from A0 + A1), with the same metadata requirements as A0.
3. **A short first-reflection note** (~ 200 words) that answers two questions: (a) what did you learn in reading-through that you did not already know? and (b) what one thing would you like to understand better before starting your track work?

## How it is scored

Raw score 0–9 → rescaled to 5 points.

### Completeness (0–3)

- **0** — Schema study missing, or < 5 additional articles submitted.
- **1** — Schema study present but brief (< 200 words), or 5–9 additional articles.
- **2** — Schema study ~ 400 words, 15 additional articles, first-reflection note present.
- **3** — Schema study > 400 words and addresses the prompt specifically, 15 articles all on-topic, first-reflection note raises a real question (not a platitude).

### Quality (0–3)

- **0** — Schema study is generic AI-generated text with no specific framework engagement; articles are sloppy or off-topic.
- **1** — Schema study names a framework but does not engage with the framework's specifics; articles meet basic metadata bar but are thematically scattered.
- **2** — Schema study engages with a specific claim of a specific framework; articles are coherent around a small topical neighbourhood (not random).
- **3** — Schema study shows evidence the student read at least one of the cited references end-to-end; articles form a defensible sweep of a coherent sub-topic (e.g. "prospect-refuge in office design" or "soundscape effects on stress recovery").

### Reflection (0–3)

- **0** — No reflection note, or a reflection that says nothing substantive.
- **1** — A reflection that lists topics but does not articulate what was learned.
- **2** — A reflection that identifies one specific thing the student did not previously know, and one specific question.
- **3** — A reflection that connects the new-learning to the student's prior academic work or practical interest and articulates the question in a form a research supervisor could actually respond to.

## Verification procedure (TA)

Run the Track-2 automated query filtered to `submitted_by = {student_id}` and `submitted_at BETWEEN week2_start AND week3_end`. Confirm total across A0+A1 is ≥ 20 rows.

Manual check: read the schema study and the first-reflection note in the shared doc. The schema study should score 0–3 on its own against the Quality band above; the reflection note against the Reflection band.

Estimated grading time per student: 10–12 minutes.

## Late / missing policy

A1 is foundational to the track start. Late submissions receive − 1 on the rescaled score per day late, floored at 0 after 4 days. The Week-3 planning meeting expects everyone to have A1 in hand, so students who miss it will be flagged for track-lead attention.
