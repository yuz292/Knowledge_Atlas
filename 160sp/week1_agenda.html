What This Course Is and How AI Actually Works in Practice

This class teaches you to direct AI systems toward production-quality knowledge infrastructure. The content domain is environmental psychology — how physical spaces shape cognition, emotion, and behavior — but the methodology transfers to any domain. Knowledge Atlas is the practice substrate: a real system with real data, real users, and real gaps that need closing.

The Seven AI Failure Modes

AI systems fail in predictable, recurring patterns. Knowing these patterns is the difference between producing work you can trust and producing work that looks right but is subtly wrong. The first five failures emerge in single prompts; the last two — Deletion and Drift — are slow-acting and compound silently over long sessions.

1. Plausible Hallucination

Content that reads correctly, uses domain vocabulary, and is completely fabricated. Fake citations, invented effect sizes.

2. Wrong-Problem Drift

Starts on task X, gradually migrates to easier task Y. Common in multi-step prompts where attention degrades.

3. Self-Congratulatory Report

AI implements, tests its own work, and reports success — but the tests verify what the code does, not what it should do.

4. Consensus Fabrication

When synthesizing perspectives, the AI smooths over disagreements — destroying the information that matters most.

5. Premature Abstraction

"Several studies found..." instead of "3 studies (Mehta 2012, N=45; Steidle 2013, N=80) found..."

6. Attention-Induced Deletion

AI focuses on a subset of files and silently drops everything outside its attention window. Repos shrink instead of growing.

7. Session Drift

Over a long session the AI loses the original goal. Each step looks reasonable, but the cumulative trajectory diverges. A 12-line fix expands to 3,200 lines across 14 files.

The methodology exists because every shortcut has been tried and has failed

The seven-step workflow (Define → Panel → Spec → Plan → Implement → Verify → Iterate) is not bureaucracy. It is the minimum structure that prevents these failure modes from silently ruining your work. Students who skip the panel step produce systems that solve the wrong problem. Students who skip the spec step produce systems that cannot be tested.

2

Learn the Search Method

Walk through the Google AI Citation Guide together. Standard academic search uses Boolean operators and treats retrieval as keyword matching. Google AI Citation treats it as question answering — the language model interprets your query semantically and surfaces papers that address the specific intersection of concepts you describe, even when they do not contain your exact keywords.

3

Begin Searching (Remaining Class Time)

Open Google Scholar, paste your first question, and start evaluating results. For each paper: check that it is peer-reviewed, relevant to the question, and available as a PDF. Download the PDF. Record the APA citation and DOI. Aim for 3–5 papers per question by the end of class.

Tip: Quality over quantity

A paper is only useful if it directly addresses your question. A tangentially related paper wastes your time and clutters the corpus. When in doubt, read the abstract. If the abstract does not mention the specific environmental variable and outcome in your question, move on.

By end of Thursday
