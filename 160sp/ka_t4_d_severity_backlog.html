◎Goalwhat this deliverable teaches

You will take the union of findings from T4.a (heuristic audit) and T4.c (usability pilot), deduplicate them, rate each on Nielsen's five-level severity scale, and emit a ranked backlog using a RICE-style score (Reach · Impact · Confidence · Effort). The output is a single backlog.csv that the T4.f redesign deliverable consumes as input: the top three rows drive three of your proposed redesigns.

The craft here is defending severity ratings with evidence. A naive backlog rates everything 3/5 because 3 is the middle; a band-3 backlog spans at least four severity levels and names, for each rating, the exact pilot quote or audit observation that justifies it.

›What you'll dothe steps

  1. Merge your T4.a findings (10 rows) and T4.c recurring findings (typically 5–12 rows) into a single CSV. Deduplicate exact matches; near-duplicates become one row with both sources cited.
  2. For each finding, fill five fields: severity (1–5), reach (how many of the three archetypes hit this), impact (1–3; task-failure = 3), confidence (1–3; "seen in pilot twice" = 3, "plausible from audit only" = 1), effort (S / M / L — your estimate of redesign work).
  3. Compute a RICE score: (reach × impact × confidence) / effort_weight where effort_weight is 1 for S, 2 for M, 4 for L. Higher is better.
  4. Sort descending by RICE. The top three rows become the input to T4.f.
  5. Write a 200-word backlog_notes.md explaining the three top rows: why these three, why in this order, what is the lowest-severity finding you deliberately chose not to prioritise and why that was defensible.
  6. Submit backlog.csv and backlog_notes.md under 160sp/tracks/t4/{your_id}/backlog/.

✗Example of a naive approachso you can see what scores low

The classic weak backlog is a CSV where the severity column is the moderator's affect rating rather than a severity rating. Read the excerpt below and note that every severity has drifted into "how annoyed was I" rather than "how much did this block the user."

band 0–1 example — do NOT submit a backlog like this 
id,title,source,severity,reach,impact,confidence,effort,rice
F-01,home button hard to see,audit,4,?,?,?,?,?
F-02,topic page is weird,pilot,3,?,?,?,?,?
F-03,navbar colours not my favourite,audit,3,?,?,?,?,?
F-04,could not find login,pilot,5,?,?,?,?,?
F-05,footer link broken on Safari,audit,2,?,?,?,?,?
Why this scores band 0 or 1. Every weakness in this backlog is a single-word pattern. "Topic page is weird" is not a finding, it is a mood; the severity rating of 3 has no evidence column pointing back to a specific pilot quote or audit observation. "Navbar colours not my favourite" is aesthetic preference rated at severity 3, which implies a task-blocker; Nielsen's own rubric reserves the middle of the scale for "major usability problem — important to fix" and cosmetic issues belong at severity 1. The "could not find login" row is rated 5 but has no reach/impact/confidence data, which means the ranking is literally a guess; a reviewer cannot tell whether this was observed once or five times, in which archetype, or whether the moderator just pattern-matched on "login failure". The unfilled reach/impact/confidence/effort columns mean no RICE score can be computed, so no defensible ranking exists — which means the backlog cannot drive T4.f. Band 3 requires every numeric cell filled and a one-sentence evidence note for every severity of 3 or higher.

The backlog-row template. Every row in backlog.csv:

id, title, source, severity(1–5), reach(1–3), impact(1–3),
confidence(1–3), effort(S|M|L), rice, evidence_ref

F-01, "Topic-page chip labels use internal jargon (defended/mixed/unresolved)",
  "audit:T4.a-04 + pilot:S2,S4", 4, 3, 3, 3, M, 13.5,
  "S2 7:18 'is defended a fencing thing?'; S4 12:02 same confusion"

The evidence_ref column is the load-bearing one. It is the link between pilot notes and severity rating; without it the rating is unfalsifiable.

✓Verification

how your work is graded
  • Automated: the AI grader parses backlog.csv, checks all columns are present, and verifies the RICE column is consistent with the formula given.
  • Automated: findings at severity ≥ 3 without an evidence_ref string cap Quality at band 1.
  • Automated: the top-3 rows are extracted and handed to T4.f as input; the three redesigns in T4.f must reference these IDs.
  • TA audit: a TA re-rates a sampled row and flags if the student's rating is > 2 bands off with no evidence to justify it.

Scoring bands (0–3 on each criterion, rescaled to the 5-point value):
