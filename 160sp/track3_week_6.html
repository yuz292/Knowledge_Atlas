Week 6

Question for the week: How do you map non-everyday research requests onto transformations in a way that is usable?

Use AI well

Before you ask AI for help, make sure you can state what you are trying to learn this week and what evidence would count as a useful answer.

Class meetings

Meeting A — Build the translation layer
Start here: Bring one difficult instruction that sounds meaningful but is not yet directly actionable.

In class

  • Collect difficult instructions such as 'increase mystery while preserving navigability' or 'increase awe without making the room cold'.
  • Sort each instruction into what is directly actionable, what is ambiguous, and what requires a follow-up question or a default assumption.
  • Ask AI for alternative phrasings and hidden assumptions. Keep only the parts that improve your factor mapping or error handling.
  • Design a simple translation layer: accepted terms, synonyms, warnings, and structured output.

Home

  • Create a small bank of translation examples from research language to factor specs.
  • Write error messages or clarification prompts for requests that are too vague.
  • Prepare three cases where your system should decline to guess.
Meeting B — Review the translation quality
Start here: Test whether another team can understand your instruction mapping without explanation.

In class

  • Swap translation examples across teams and test whether another team can understand and act on them.
  • Revise your glossary and factor maps based on where confusion appears.
  • Decide what your system will support by final week and what it will state as unsupported or provisional.

Home

  • Clean up the translation layer documentation.
  • Pair each example request with the factor changes it produces and the assumptions it makes.
  • Make a concise user-facing explanation of the supported instruction style.

Week deliverables

D9 Prompt-to-factor translation pack

Rationale
This pack is what makes the project researcher-facing rather than tool-facing. It shows how non-everyday language becomes structured edits.

What to submit
A compact glossary, a set of supported instruction patterns, at least eight worked translation examples, and a short policy for ambiguity, default assumptions, and refusal to guess.

What good work looks like
The pack is useful to a non-expert. It does not pretend to understand everything. It shows the path from language to parameters.

Example
A worked example states that 'increase refuge while preserving visibility control' maps to moderate alcove depth, maintained sightline to door, and no reduction in overall navigable width below a stated minimum.
