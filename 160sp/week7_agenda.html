Tuesday · Day 13 · Integration Testing

Integration Testing Across Tracks

Knowledge Atlas is not four independent tracks. It is a unified system where each track's output feeds into another track's input. The Image Tagger produces tagged images. The Article Finder produces vetted articles. The VR Production track converts articles into spatial experiences. The GUI Evaluation track measures how well the interface serves users. Today you discover whether these pieces actually fit together.

1

Cross-Track Integration Test

Each track's Sprint 2 output feeds into at least one other track. You will perform the integration test: does Track 2's article data feed correctly into Track 1's tagging interface? Does Track 4's GUI correctly display Track 1's tagged images? Does the data format produced by one track match the data format expected by the next track? If not, who needs to change their code — the producer or the consumer? This is where you discover contract misalignment.

2

Fix Integration Failures

The most common problem at integration boundaries is vocabulary mismatch. Track 1's AI produces a field called "environmental_variable" while Track 2's AI produces a field called "env_factor". Both teams built exactly what they specified. Both implementations are correct. But they cannot talk to each other. This is Failure Mode #4 (Consensus Fabrication) manifesting at the system level — each track's AI smoothed over the vocabulary differences without noticing they were different in the first place. Fix this by agreeing on canonical field names and updating both implementations to use them.

Integration exposes contract gaps

When all four tracks are combined, gaps in the specification become obvious. These are not failures of individual tracks. They are failures of the overall architecture. Fixing them requires cross-track coordination.

By end of Tuesday

Integration tests completed for all track boundaries. Failures documented. Fixes implemented or agreed upon.

Thursday · Day 14 · Sprint 2 Demos

Sprint 2 Demos and Quarter Review

This is the final checkpoint before the course shifts to handoff and transition. You will present Sprint 2 work, demonstrate that your component integrates with other tracks, and reflect on what you learned about directing AI systems across the full arc of the quarter.

1

Sprint 2 Demos (5 minutes per team)

Show what changed from Sprint 1 to Sprint 2. What new capabilities did you add? What edge cases did you handle? How does your output flow into the next track's input? What integration failures did you discover and fix? What surprised you? The demo should show the end-to-end system working with real data, not abstract descriptions.

2

Quarter Methodology Review (20 minutes)

Step back and look across all eight weeks of methodology logs. What patterns emerge? Which failure modes were most common? Which techniques were most effective for directing AI? What would you do differently if you could do the quarter again? Which decisions would you keep, and which would you reverse? This conversation is not about the tracks — it is about the methodology itself. You are providing feedback on the process that guided your work.

Methodology is the real deliverable

The code, the data, the interfaces — these are important. But the methodology log is the artifact that matters most. It documents how you directed AI, what worked, what failed, and what you learned. The next cohort will study these logs before they begin their own work.
