Can your persona panel distinguish between pages that work for your user and pages that fail them — and can you explain why?

Why this week matters

A panel that praises everything or condemns everything is poorly constructed. The calibration triad forces your panel to make discriminations: one page you expect to work, one you expect to fail, one you are genuinely uncertain about. After running all three, you know whether your panel is generating real signal or just narrative.

The calibration triad

Pick three pages from your assigned cluster: (1) one you predict will work well for your persona, (2) one you predict will frustrate them, (3) one where you are genuinely unsure. Run your panel on all three. If the panel agrees with all your predictions, be suspicious — it may be echoing your expectations rather than testing them.

What you need to understand before building

Journey ≠ click sequence

A journey documents the user's goal, their mental model at each step, where expectations broke, and what they did next. A click sequence is just a log. You need the journey — it includes the reasoning, not just the clicks.

Friction inventories are the unit of currency

Every page walkthrough produces a friction inventory: a table with columns for Page, Step, Friction Description, Nielsen Heuristic Violated, Severity (cosmetic/minor/major/catastrophic), and Proposed Fix. These tables accumulate across weeks and drive the fix work in Week 6.

Panel disagreement is data

When your three panellists disagree — one finds the page clear, another finds it confusing — that disagreement maps to real variation among users. Document it. Do not resolve it by majority vote.

Persona calibration memo

After running the triad, write a one-page memo: (a) where did the panel's reactions surprise you, (b) what did the panellists disagree about and why, (c) what would you revise about the persona description itself based on these walkthroughs?

How to use AI this week

Ask AI for

  • journey structure templates — "give me a 10-step journey template for a researcher looking for effect sizes"
  • friction classification — "which Nielsen heuristic does this violation fall under?"
  • severity calibration — "is this a minor or major violation given a 3-minute time budget?"
  • cross-page comparison — "the panel praised page A but condemned page B; what structural difference explains this?"

Do not ask AI to

  • pick your triad pages for you — the choice itself is part of the exercise
  • write the calibration memo without you reflecting on what surprised you
  • collapse panel disagreements into a single verdict

In-class activities

Tuesday — Calibration triad
Start here: Name your three triad pages and your prediction for each (works / fails / uncertain).

In class

  • Run your panel on all three triad pages.
  • Build a friction inventory table for each page.
  • Note where the panel surprised you — these are the most informative moments.

Home

  • Write the persona calibration memo (one page).
  • Revise your persona description if the triad revealed gaps.
Thursday — Journey drafting
Start here: Share your calibration memo. What surprised you?

In class

  • Draft two complete user journeys for your persona, each with 5–12 steps.
  • Each journey has: entry point, goal, expected path, actual path (from panel), friction points at each step, and a satisfaction rating.
  • Compare journeys with a partner who holds a different persona — identify where your personas' needs conflict.

Home

  • Clean up both journey documents with full friction inventory tables.
  • File as PRs to docs/ux/journeys/.

Week deliverables

D4 Persona calibration memo

Rationale
The memo proves you can evaluate your own evaluation instrument. A panel you never calibrate is a tool you do not understand.

What to submit
One-page memo answering: (a) surprises, (b) disagreements, (c) persona revisions.

What good work looks like
Specific about what the panel got wrong or right. Revises the persona with concrete details, not hand-waving.

Example
"Panel 2 (the sceptic) correctly predicted Maya would miss the breadcrumb, but overestimated her willingness to read the sidebar. Revised Maya's time budget from 5 min to 3 min per page."

D5 Two journey documents

Rationale
Journey maps are the diagnostic artefact that drives the fix work in Weeks 6–7. Without them, you are guessing what to fix.

What to submit
Two journey documents, each with a friction inventory table (Page, Step, Friction, Heuristic, Severity, Fix). Filed as PRs to docs/ux/journeys/.

What good work looks like
Journeys are grounded in panel walkthroughs, not speculation. Friction points cite specific heuristics. Severity ratings are justified.

Example
Journey: "Dr. Okafor searches for open-plan cortisol evidence." Steps: arrives at ka_home_researcher.html → hypothesis-test workflow → searches → finds article card → clicks through → looks for effect size → cannot find sample size → leaves."
