Seven Responses to Anomalous Data

Chinn & Brewer (1993) catalogued how readers actually respond when presented with evidence that conflicts with a theory they hold. Their seven-response menu is descriptive, not prescriptive: it tells journey designers what readers do, not what they should do. Use it to anticipate how a reader will receive the evidence on a journey page, and to surface the response options explicitly rather than letting them happen invisibly.

Anomalous datum (worked example)

A pre-registered replication finds no effect of biophilic indoor plants on perceived restorativeness in office workers, despite a large meta-analytic effect in earlier studies.

theory-protective  ·  theory-flexible  ·  theory-revising
