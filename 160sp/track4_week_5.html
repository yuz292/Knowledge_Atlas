Why this week matters

Journey maps are narrative. Heuristic audits are systematic. You need both. The audit forces you to evaluate every page in your cluster against the same 35 criteria, which prevents you from fixating on the first problem you noticed and missing the pattern that connects twelve smaller problems across pages.

Prioritise by severity × frequency

A major violation that appears on 12 pages matters more than a catastrophic violation on one obscure page. The audit report must rank findings, not just list them.

The 35-dimension framework

The K-ATLAS usability critic uses three established frameworks. You do not need to memorise all 35 dimensions, but you must apply them systematically to each page.

Nielsen's 10 usability heuristics (H1–H10)

IDHeuristicOne-line test
H1Visibility of system statusDoes the user always know where they are and what is happening?
H2Match between system and real worldDoes the language match the user's mental model?
H3User control and freedomCan the user undo, go back, or escape?
H4Consistency and standardsDo similar things look and behave the same way?
H5Error preventionDoes the design prevent mistakes before they happen?
H6Recognition rather than recallIs information visible rather than requiring memory?
H7Flexibility and efficiency of useCan experts take shortcuts?
H8Aesthetic and minimalist designIs every element earning its space?
H9Help users recognise and recover from errorsAre error messages clear and actionable?
H10Help and documentationIs help available and findable when needed?

Shneiderman's 8 golden rules (R1–R8)

IDRuleOne-line test
R1Strive for consistencySame layout, colour, and terminology across pages?
R2Seek universal usabilityWorks for novices and experts? Accessible?
R3Offer informative feedbackEvery action gets a visible response?
R4Design dialogs to yield closureMulti-step tasks have clear beginning, middle, and end?
R5Prevent errorsInvalid states are unreachable?
R6Permit easy reversal of actionsCan the user get back to where they were?
R7Keep users in controlThe user initiates actions, not the system?
R8Reduce short-term memory loadInformation from one step is still visible in the next?

Visualization dimensions (V1–V17)

For pages with charts, graphs, or data displays, use this framework:

IDDimensionOne-line test
V1Data-ink ratioDoes every pixel encode data, or are there decorative elements?
V2Lie factorDo graphical proportions match the underlying numbers?
V3Colour accessibilityIs the palette usable for colour-blind viewers?
V4Axis labellingAre axis labels clear, complete, and unambiguous?
V5Legend clarityCan the user map legend items back to the chart without searching?
V6Tooltip helpfulnessDo tooltips add information or just repeat what is visible?
V7Responsive sizingDoes the chart remain readable on mobile and desktop?
V8Annotation qualityDo annotations clarify or distract from the main pattern?
V9Perceptual orderingAre elements ordered by the viewer's natural perceptual hierarchy?
V10Small-multiples coherenceDo multiple charts use consistent scales and layouts?
V11Animation appropriatenessDoes animation reveal patterns or just distract?
V12Drill-down affordanceIs it clear that the user can interact or explore further?
V13Uncertainty representationAre confidence intervals, error bars, or ranges shown where needed?
V14Comparison supportCan the user compare values across series or time periods easily?
V15Label readabilityAre labels legible in size, contrast, and font?
V16Interactive feedbackDoes the interface respond immediately to user actions?
V17Export capabilityCan the user save or export the data or visualization?

How to use AI this week

Ask AI for

  • heuristic-by-heuristic evaluation — paste page HTML, ask "evaluate against H1 through H10"
  • accessibility audit — "run axe-core checks on this markup and list WCAG violations"
  • pattern detection — "I found H4 violations on 5 pages; what underlying CSS inconsistency could explain all five?"
  • severity justification — "is this a cosmetic or major violation for a researcher with 30 minutes?"

Do not ask AI to

  • produce the entire audit report without you verifying each finding on the live page
  • rate severity without specifying which persona and time budget
  • declare a page "passes" all heuristics — every page has violations; the question is how severe

Week deliverables

D6 Heuristic audit report

Rationale
The audit is the diagnostic foundation for everything that follows. Without it, fixes are guided by taste rather than evidence.

What to submit
A structured report covering at least 8 pages. One section per page, each with a friction inventory table. Summary section ranking findings by severity × frequency.

What good work looks like
Findings cite specific heuristics with evidence (screenshots, quotes from panel transcripts). Severity ratings are justified by persona context. The priority ranking is defensible.

Example
"H4 violation: the sidebar on ka_articles.html uses a different link colour (#2A7868) than the sidebar on ka_topics.html (#1A5A50). Severity: minor (cosmetic inconsistency, but erodes trust across repeated visits). Frequency: appears on 8 of 13 content-nav pages."
