Tuesday · Day 7 · Track Launch

Track Deep-Dive and Sprint 1 Launch

This week marks the transition from theory to production. You have completed your orientation, understood the methodology, collected articles, and chosen your track. Today you receive a track-specific technical orientation, review your approved contract one final time, and begin Step 5 of the seven-step workflow: Implementation. The methodology is no longer abstract — it is the structure that governs your work from this moment forward.

1

Track-Specific Technical Orientation

Each track has its own vocabulary, tools, and data structures. In this thirty-minute session, we walk through the tools and context files specific to your track. You will learn the exact data schemas you are building against, the acceptance criteria you must satisfy, and the interface contracts that bind your component to the rest of the system. If you are on the Image Tagger track, you will understand the image metadata structure and the tagging interface protocol. If you are on the Article Finder track, you will understand the query format and retrieval architecture. The context file for your track contains the AI vocabulary — this is what you load into your AI system to constrain hallucination and enforce domain consistency.

2

Sprint 1 Implementation Begins

After orientation, you move to Step 5 of the workflow. Your specification is already approved (from Week 3). You have your contract, your context file, and your AI system configured with the correct role and domain knowledge. From this moment until Thursday, your task is to implement the specification. Begin by loading the context file and the acceptance criteria into your AI system. Review what you committed to building. Break the implementation into smaller tasks and work through them sequentially. Document each significant decision in your methodology log.

Remember the methodology

Define, Panel, Spec, Plan, Implement, Verify, Iterate. You already have your spec from Week 3. Today you plan and begin implementing. Do not skip verification — it is where failure modes surface and where you discover that your implementation solved the wrong problem or used the wrong data format. Verification is not something you do at the end: you build verification into your work from the beginning.

Thursday · Day 8 · Build + Test

Build, Test, Submit Progress

You have three days to move from specification to working code. Thursday is the checkpoint: you should have a working first draft, passing tests against your acceptance criteria, and an updated methodology log that documents what you built, what you tried, what failed, and how you recovered. This is not about finishing Sprint 1 — it is about knowing where you stand and what remains.

1

Continue Implementation

You have been working since Tuesday morning. Continue implementing the remaining tasks. If you are stuck on something, document the blocker: what did you try, what failed, what is the next strategy. The goal is a working first draft by the end of class. It does not need to be perfect — it needs to run and produce output that you can test against your acceptance criteria.

2

Run Verification Against Your Contract

Open your acceptance criteria. For each criterion, test your implementation: does it pass or fail? Document every result. If a test fails, write up what you expected and what actually happened. This is not a failure — it is data. The discrepancy between what you built and what you promised to build tells you exactly what you need to fix in the next phase.

3

Update Your Methodology Log

Record the following: the prompts you gave to your AI system, the outputs you received, the failures you encountered, how you corrected them, and what assumptions you made. This log is the artifact that proves you followed the methodology. It is also the institutional memory that the next cohort will learn from. If you made a mistake and caught it during verification, document that mistake and how you fixed it. That is the most valuable kind of entry.

Due: End of Thursday

Sprint 1 progress checkpoint: working draft with test results, documentation of what passes and what fails, updated methodology log. Full Sprint 1 due end of Week 5.
