How submissions are recorded

Submissions are recorded automatically when you open a Pull Request on GitHub from your track1/<your-name> branch into dkirsh:main. The PR creation timestamp becomes the “Submitted at” entry; the grade and critique link appear after the instructor finishes reviewing.

If a row stays empty after you have opened a PR, the registry has not yet picked up your branch — refresh after fifteen minutes. If it still does not appear, message the instructor on the PR thread.

Late or revised submissions. If you push additional commits after a grade is posted, the page shows the most recent submission timestamp and a small history of prior submissions for the same task. Grades are only regenerated when the instructor explicitly re-grades the PR; do not assume a push triggers a re-grade.

What “graded” means here

Each task is graded against the rubric on its own page, with the contract gate as a hard prerequisite. Numerical scores reflect the rubric line items; the comments/critique link points at the PR conversation where the instructor records what worked, what needs revision, and what the work taught us about the AI-assisted process. Treat the comments as the substantive feedback — the number is the summary, not the substance.
