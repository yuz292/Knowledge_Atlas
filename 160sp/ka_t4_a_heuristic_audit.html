◎Goalwhat this deliverable teaches

You will produce ten structured findings against the K-Atlas public site, each one coded to one of Nielsen's ten usability heuristics. By the end you will be able to tell a design-decision story in the language any working UX team uses: heuristic · scenario · observed · expected · severity · recommendation.

This deliverable is medium-hardness because the art is not in finding problems — the current site has many — but in writing findings that another student could independently reproduce without seeing your screenshots. A finding that only you can interpret scores band 1; a finding that reproduces in someone else's browser scores band 3.

›What you'll dothe steps

  1. Read Nielsen's ten heuristics. Keep the list open in a tab; you will cite each finding against exactly one heuristic.
  2. Walk the eight pages listed in the Files and links section below. Spend 5–10 minutes on each page with a task in mind (the page-specific task is suggested inline).
  3. When you notice friction, stop and record a finding in the schema below. Do not try to fix anything on the live site. Take a screenshot, note what you expected vs. what happened.
  4. Produce ten findings total, covering at least six distinct heuristics. Severity ratings should span at least three levels (not all level 2; if your severities all land in the middle, you are not being discriminating enough).
  5. Save each finding as its own markdown file under 160sp/tracks/t4/{your_id}/findings/T4.a_{nn}.md using the schema in the naive-approach block below.
  6. Submit by opening a short GitHub issue tagging T4.a with the list of your ten finding filenames.

✗Example of a naive approachso you can see what scores low

A common first attempt looks like the block below. It documents a real friction point, but the finding is unreproducible, uncoded, and actionless. Use it as a counter-example; the "why this scores low" annotation after it maps each weakness back to the rubric.

band 0–1 example — do NOT submit findings like this

T4.a-01 · Home page is confusing

The home page is really confusing and I could not figure out what to do. The buttons are weird and nothing makes sense. This is a bad page. 4/5.

Why this scores band 0 or 1. The finding fails every structural requirement at once: no heuristic is named (we cannot tell whether this is a Match between system and the real world problem or a Recognition rather than recall problem); no scenario is declared (which user doing what task?); the observed behaviour is subjective ("confusing") rather than literal; there is no expected behaviour for comparison; no screenshot; no recommendation; severity is picked from the air. A reviewer cannot reproduce the problem and a designer cannot act on it. Band 3 requires all seven schema fields, each filled with specific and defensible content.

The schema a good finding follows. Copy this into each T4.a_{nn}.md file and fill in every field:

# T4.a-{nn} — {short title}

**Heuristic**: {one of Nielsen's 10, e.g. "H4 Consistency and standards"}
**Scenario**: {one-line scenario the user was attempting}
**Page**: {URL}
**Observed behaviour**: {literal description, 30–80 words}
**Expected behaviour**: {what would make the scenario succeed, 20–50 words}
**Impact**: {who is affected and how, 20–40 words}
**Severity**: {1–5 per Nielsen}
**Screenshot**: {path to ./screenshots/T4.a_{nn}.png}
**Recommendation**: {one-paragraph suggested fix, 40–80 words}

✓Verification

how your work is graded
  • Automated: the AI grader reads every T4.a_{nn}.md file in your findings/ directory and checks the schema is complete. Missing sections cap the Completeness band at 1.
  • Automated: the 10 findings must cover ≥ 6 distinct heuristics for band 3 on Quality; severity ratings must span ≥ 3 levels.
  • TA audit: 20% of submissions get a blind re-rating by a TA; large TA/AI disagreement flags the deliverable for re-review.
  • Peer reproducibility: your findings are the direct input to T4.e, where a paired student tries to reproduce 5 of them. A finding that no peer can reproduce is not a finding.

Scoring bands at a glance (0–3 on each criterion, rescaled to the 12-point deliverable value):

CriterionBand 0Band 1Band 2Band 3
Completeness < 5 findings, or schema broken 5–7 findings, schema partial 8–9 findings, schema complete 10 findings, schema complete, screenshots present
Quality Findings reproduce nowhere Weak coverage (1–3 heuristics) Reasonable coverage (4–5 heuristics) ≥ 6 heuristics, severities span ≥ 3 levels, every observation is literal
Reflection No reflection note Generic reflection Names two specific patterns Names two patterns and proposes one systemic fix
