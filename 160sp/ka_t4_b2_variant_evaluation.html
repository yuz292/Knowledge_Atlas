◎Goal

The Knowledge Atlas ships six topic-page variants — the same underlying crosswalk data rendered as six different interaction patterns. You will walk each of your three T4.b archetypes through each of the six variants (15 walkthroughs), record timings and friction per walkthrough, and produce a defended design recommendation for which variant should become the site's default topic page.

This is a real evaluation whose output will inform a production decision. The design recommendation in your comparison report goes straight to the instructor's short-list for the default ka_topics.html entry.

›What you'll do

  1. Open your three T4.b archetype files. If the scenarios are not sharp, sharpen them first (a vague scenario produces vague walkthroughs).
  2. For each of the 15 cells (3 archetypes × 5 new variants, plus 3 × 1 Classic View = 15 total when you count the Classic View separately; we count the six variants), open the variant in a fresh browser tab, execute the archetype's primary scenario, and record what happens.
  3. Use the walkthrough-matrix form below to capture every cell. Measure click count and rough time per scenario with a stopwatch. Do not simulate the walkthrough mentally — run it.
  4. After the matrix is full, identify 5–7 findings using the T4 finding schema. Each finding must cite the matrix rows that produced it.
  5. Write an 850–1,100-word comparison report with the six sections in the rubric (scenarios summary, per-archetype preference, cross-cutting patterns, per-variant strengths, design recommendation, reflection).
  6. Submit the package as 160sp/tracks/t4/{your_id}/T4.b.2/ per the rubric's § 2.

✗Example of a naive approach

band 0–1 example — walkthroughs without differentiation

A walkthrough matrix that shows the student did not actually walk through the variants: all 15 rows report clicks=3, all report time=10s, the first_friction_point column is empty or reads "none", and the notes column says "worked fine" for every cell.

Why this scores low. The six variants are deliberately different — the Heatmap does not even have a click path to a topic bundle in the way the Facet View does; the Quick Lookup composes filters differently from the Full Facets. If your matrix is flat, you did not walk through. The T4.b.2 design recommendation rests on differentiated data; a matrix with no variance across variants produces a recommendation of the form "they all work fine", which cannot be defended.

✓Verification

grading
  • Automated: all 18 matrix cells (3 archetypes × 6 variants) are populated; each cell has click-count, time, first-friction-point, and notes.
  • Automated: 5–7 findings exist in T4.b.2/findings/, each citing matrix rows; comparison report is 850–1,100 words with all six required sections.
  • Quality check: the AI grader spot-checks three cells and confirms the timing + click data is internally consistent with the notes; a matrix that is flat across variants caps Quality at band 1.
  • Realism check: the design recommendation must name a specific variant (or a specific hybrid with named affordances) for band 3; generic recommendations cap at band 1 or 2.
