Claim one of the 8 personas

3 · Panel

Build your AI persona panel

4 · Evidence

Produce a journey note with at least three friction points, each tagged to a Nielsen heuristic. File one finding as a GitHub Issue with the ux-journey label.

The shape of the quarter

The track has three phases: learn to see through your persona's eyes (Weeks 3–4), diagnose what is broken and why (Week 5), and fix it with evidence that the fix worked (Weeks 6–7).

Phase 1

Persona grounding & journey mapping

Weeks 3.5–4. You build AI persona panels, calibrate them against multiple pages, and produce detailed journey maps for your persona's tasks on the site.

Phase 2

Heuristic audits

Week 5. You systematically evaluate your assigned page cluster against 35 heuristic dimensions (Nielsen H1–H10, Shneiderman R1–R8, Visualization V1–V17). Deliverable: a prioritised audit report.

Phase 3

Fix sprints & integration

Weeks 6–7. You implement your top-severity fixes as Pull Requests, re-run your persona panel to measure improvement, and present your work in a 10-minute assertion-evidence slide deck.

Page clusters & ownership

The site has roughly 120 pages across several natural clusters. Each student owns the cluster(s) most relevant to their persona.

Cluster A — Content navigation

~13 pages: articles, topics, theories, mechanisms, neuro perspective, search, filtering, card views. Best fit: Researcher or Theory Explorer persona.

Cluster B — Portals & identity

~9 pages: global home, 6 user-type portals, login, student profile. Best fit: PI or cross-cutting reviewer.

Cluster C — COGS 160 student pages

~68 pages: schedule, track hubs, weekly pages, exercises, assignments, studio pages. Best fit: Student Explorer persona.

Cluster D — Research & theory tools

~10 pages: article detail, argumentation/interpretation panels, QA, warrant displays, theory comparison. Best fit: Researcher persona.

Cluster E — Visualizations

~9 pages with charting libraries (Chart.js, D3): network graphs, evidence distributions, topic maps. Best fit: Practitioner persona.

Cluster F — Designing Experiments

~12 pages in the Designing_Experiments/ subdirectory with its own navigation. Best fit: Researcher or Contributor persona.

How to work with AI in this track

Ask AI for

  • persona panel construction — three panellists with specific contexts, goals, and knowledge states
  • heuristic evaluation — "apply Nielsen H4 (consistency) to this page and list violations"
  • journey simulation — "walk this page as Dr. Okafor looking for effect sizes on open-plan cortisol"
  • fix proposals — "suggest CSS/JS changes that would address this accessibility violation"
  • comparative critique — "compare the navigation on these two pages and identify inconsistencies"

Do not ask AI to

  • declare a page "good" or "bad" without specifying which heuristics and for which persona
  • generate journey observations without anchoring to a specific URL you have actually visited
  • produce a heuristic report without you verifying each finding against the real page
  • write your fix PR without you understanding what the code change does
