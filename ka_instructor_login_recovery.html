What to do when a student still cannot sign in

Use this page as the standard procedure. The point is to stop guessing, avoid duplicate accounts, and apply the smallest fix that restores the student's access.

Most Common

Student says: “I forgot my password”

Use the manual reset tool. This is the default remedy when mail delivery is unreliable.

Registration

Student sees: “That email is not registered”

Determine whether the account truly does not exist, or whether the student is using the wrong address.

Duplicate Account Risk

Student sees: “An account with that email already exists”

Do not register again. Treat this as an existing-account recovery case.

Not Password

Student can sign in, but Question 1 or Question 2 is wrong or blank

This is an assignment-state problem, not a password problem. Keep the same account and inspect the assignment page.

1

Before you reset anything

Ask the student for the exact address they entered, the exact message they saw, and whether they still control that email inbox.

  • Have them copy the address exactly, not from memory.
  • Ask for a screenshot of the message if possible.
  • Confirm whether they ever successfully signed in before.
Do not guess which email they used. Most login confusion is one of two things: wrong address or existing account with forgotten password.
2

If the student forgot the password, or reset email is not arriving

Use the manual reset tool on the instructor review page.

  • Open Instructor Review.
  • In Manual Password Reset, enter the student's registered email.
  • Generate the one-time reset link.
  • Verify the student's identity before sending it.
  • Send the link by a trusted channel you already use, such as the verified course email thread or a trusted phone number from course records.
  • After the student signs in again, send them to Account Settings if they need to change email or password.
This is the normal fallback when server email is down. The system itself does not send SMS.
3

If the student sees “That email is not registered”

Separate the true first-time user from the returning user who is typing the wrong address.

  • If the student has never registered, send them to Register.
  • If the student previously used K-ATLAS, do not create a second account.
  • Ask them which address they used originally. Common mistakes are a personal address instead of UCSD, or a spelling variation.
  • If they remain uncertain, treat it as an existing-account recovery case and use the manual reset workflow on the likely registered address.
Never solve this by creating duplicate accounts for the same student unless you are certain there was no prior registration.
4

If the student sees “An account with that email already exists”

The account already exists. The question is no longer registration; it is access recovery.

  • Send them to Sign In if they remember the password.
  • If they do not remember it, use the manual reset link workflow.
  • After access is restored, use Account Settings to correct the email if the account is attached to the wrong address.
5

If the student can sign in but the course assignment is wrong or blank

This is no longer a login failure. Keep the same account and inspect the course-assignment view.

  • Send the student to Collect Articles Upload.
  • Have them refresh the page after signing in again.
  • If the assignment is still blank or wrong, collect their exact email and a screenshot.
  • Do not create a new account to fix an assignment problem.
Wrong Question 1 or Question 2 is an assignment-state problem, not evidence that the password reset failed.
6

Rules that prevent more damage

  • Do not ask the student to keep guessing passwords indefinitely.
  • Do not send a manual reset link before you verify identity.
  • Do not create a second account just because login failed once.
  • Do not change the account email until the student has regained access.
