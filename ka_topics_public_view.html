What does the research say about architecture and people?

Knowledge Atlas indexes ~ 1,400 peer-reviewed studies on how the built environment affects human cognition, emotion, stress, attention, and well-being. We group those studies into topic bundles so you can see, for any question you care about, what the evidence actually supports and how strongly.

Below, pick a topic the way that feels most natural. You can start from an outcome you care about (stress, focus, sleep), from an architectural feature you're curious about (daylight, acoustics, greenery), or see the full landscape. How we weigh evidence →

A few words you'll see on this page. A topic bundle groups studies that look at the same kind of environmental feature (e.g. luminous = lighting / daylight) and the same kind of outcome (e.g. cog.attention = effects on attention). The pill next to a topic tells you how settled the evidence is: defended means the evidence is strong enough that we'd cite it confidently; mixed means studies disagree or the sample is thin; unresolved means we can't yet align the term with a canonical outcome vocabulary.
