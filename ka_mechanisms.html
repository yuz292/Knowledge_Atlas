Mechanisms

Proposed causal chains through which features of the environment produce cognitive, affective, or behavioural outcomes. Each entry names the chain and states its evidentiary status in one line.

Noise → cortisol → cognitive performance

Well-supported · open-plan offices, classrooms

Chronic background noise above about 55 dBA sustains cortisol elevation, which produces a measurable decrement on hippocampal-dependent memory tasks.

Daylight → circadian entrainment → alertness

Well-supported · office and residential

Morning exposure to at least 250 melanopic lux activates intrinsically photosensitive retinal ganglion cells, advancing the suprachiasmatic nucleus phase and producing daytime alertness gains.

Ceiling height → construal level → creativity

Partial support · Meyers-Levy & Zhu · 2007

High ceilings prime a "freedom" construct and raise construal level, producing modest creativity gains; original laboratory finding replicates inconsistently and field effects are small.

Thermal comfort → affect → prosocial behaviour

Speculative · Williams & Bargh · 2008 (challenged)

Warmer ambient temperature was proposed to prime a "warmth" concept that shifts prosocial judgement; large multi-lab replication efforts have failed, so the mechanism is retained here as a cautionary example.

Crowding → cognitive load → error rate

Partial support · dense residential, healthcare

Perceived social density imposes working-memory load from inhibiting social monitoring, producing increased error rates on concurrent cognitive tasks.

Materiality → thermal affect → room preference

Partial support · wood vs. metal surfaces

Touch of warm-feeling wood surfaces lowers skin-conductance response and raises positive affect, producing consistent room-preference differences relative to cool-feeling metal or stone.
