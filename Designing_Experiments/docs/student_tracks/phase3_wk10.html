The test of whether your work was meaningful is the re-walkthrough. In Week 8 you documented what was broken. In Week 9 you fixed what you could. Now you repeat the simulation from scratch — fresh eyes — and measure the difference. If a naïve Fall student can now navigate your section without the obstacles you documented, your contribution is real. If they still cannot, you know precisely what remains and why.

The hand-off document is what the Fall instructor receives. It is the artefact that outlives your participation in this project.

Tuesday–Thursday: The Hand-Off Document

Create docs/phase3_audit/section_X/HANDOFF_REPORT.md. This document is addressed to the Fall 2026 instructor who will use the Designing_Experiments site with a new cohort of students. Its purpose is to tell them exactly what state the site is in, what works, what does not, and what would need to happen to complete any unfinished items.

Required sections of the hand-off report

  1. Section overview (1–2 paragraphs): what this section of the site is supposed to do, which pages are included, and which student track it serves.
  2. State of the site as of Week 10: a table listing every page in the section with its current functional status and the most important open issue (if any).
  3. What we fixed: a list of every fix committed during Weeks 9–10, with commit hash and a one-sentence description of the problem that was resolved.
  4. What remains open: every issue that was not resolved, with severity, the reason it was not fixed, and a recommendation for the Fall instructor (fix before Week N, defer to later, or intentional placeholder).
  5. Success conditions: copy or link to your success_conditions.md. The Fall instructor uses these to verify the site works before the quarter starts.
  6. Recommendations for improvement: the three or four most impactful changes that would improve the student experience — beyond bug fixes, into pedagogical or design improvements.

Thursday: In-Class Presentation

Presentation format: 5–8 minutes + 2 minutes Q&A

Cover the following points in order — you do not need slides, but a live browser demo is encouraged:

  1. What section were you assigned? What is it supposed to do?
  2. What were the three most critical issues you found in Week 8?
  3. Which of those did you fix? Show the diff in GitHub or in the browser.
  4. What is still broken, and why was it not fixed?
  5. What does the comparison table show — how much better is the section now?
  6. What is your top recommendation for whoever works on this section next?

Opening the Pull Request

Before Thursday class, open a PR from your Phase 3 branch to the upstream Tag-UCSD/Designing_Experiments main branch. This is how your fixes enter the canonical site.

PR checklist
