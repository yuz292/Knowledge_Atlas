How 3 Pairs Divide the Work

Each pair of 2 students specializes in one stimulus dimension from the VR design space. This allows depth specialization within a single semester while maintaining breadth across the track. All pairs follow the same timeline (Weeks 1–10), but their stimulus design choices and resulting scenes differ. This creates natural comparison across teams during peer review and final presentations.

Stimulus Dimension: Spatial Openness

Focus: How perceived spatial expansiveness (corridor vs. room vs. outdoors) influences aesthetic preference and sense of presence. Design three levels: enclosed, semi-open, fully open.
Pair 2: Pair Assignment TBD
Stimulus Dimension: Biophilic Content

Focus: How inclusion of living elements (plants, water, natural textures) impacts restorativeness and engagement. Design three levels: no nature, moderate nature, high nature saturation.
Pair 3: Pair Assignment TBD
Stimulus Dimension: Lighting Quality

Focus: How daylight characteristics (cool vs. warm color temperature, diffuse vs. direct shadows) affect mood and focus. Design three levels: cool fluorescent, warm incandescent, natural daylight.
Work Distribution Within Each Pair: One student leads literature review and design rationale; the other leads scene production and testing logistics. Both contribute to analysis and writing. Cross-Pair Coordination: All three pairs use identical held-constant parameters (room dimensions, base furniture, interaction mechanics) so that findings are comparable. A shared asset library and style guide ensures visual consistency. Alternative Stimulus Dimensions (by instructor approval): Material complexity, color saturation, avatar presence, acoustic properties, or user control options.
