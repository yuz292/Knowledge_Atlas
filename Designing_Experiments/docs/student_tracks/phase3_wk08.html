Site Assignment and First Walkthrough

Each team is assigned a section of the Designing_Experiments site to audit, test, and fix. This week: orient, claim your section on GitHub, and run the first student simulation walkthrough.

Repo: Designing_Experiments (github.com/Tag-UCSD/Designing_Experiments) Weight: 20% of final grade Due Thu: Walkthrough log pushed to your branch

What Weeks 8–10 are for. Tracks 1–4 built the research infrastructure (image tagger, article finder, VR stimuli, GUI prototype) in Weeks 3–7. Now you switch roles: instead of being a builder, you are a tester and documentarian. Your job is to simulate the experience of a student who will take the Fall 2026 version of this course — a student who has never seen these pages before — and record everything that is broken, confusing, missing, or incorrect.

The deliverable is not a critique: it is a working fix. You will leave the site section better than you found it, with every function documented and every tested path either working or explicitly flagged as not yet implemented.

The Student Simulation Walkthrough (Thursday)

On Thursday, each person on the team independently simulates the experience of a Fall 2026 student encountering your assigned section for the first time. The goal is to follow every instructed step, click every link, and attempt every task — without using any knowledge you gained building your own track. Log every obstacle precisely.

What to simulate

Start at the track overview page for your section. Attempt to complete the sequence as a student would: read the overview, navigate to the first weekly assignment, follow the instructions, access the referenced files and tools, and proceed to the next week. Stop when you reach the end of Week 7 or hit an unresolvable blocker.

Walkthrough log format

Your walkthrough_log.md must use the following entry structure for each observation:

## Observation [N]

**Page**: image_wk03_triage.html
**Step**: "Open the Tag Browser (424 tags) link in the nav"
**What happened**: Link exists but opens image_tag_table.html which shows a loading spinner
  and never populates — the JS array data appears to be missing or the file path is wrong.
**Severity**: BLOCKING (cannot complete the step without this working)
**Suggested fix**: Verify that the embedded tag data array in image_tag_table.html is intact;
  re-run the Python generation script if the data was not committed.
**Files affected**: image_tag_table.html

Severity classification

SeverityDefinitionExamples
BLOCKING Cannot complete the stated task without resolving this Broken link, missing file, JS error, empty page
MAJOR Significantly degrades understanding or usability Wrong deliverable format, unclear instructions, missing code reference
MINOR Causes confusion or requires guesswork but does not block Ambiguous wording, inconsistent nav, broken formatting
MISSING Content that is referenced but does not exist yet Week N page not created, linked file not committed, tool not available
SUGGESTION Enhancement that would improve the experience Better example, additional context, clearer heading structure

Log everything, even things that seem trivial. A MINOR issue in Week 3 that is not fixed may compound into a BLOCKING issue in Week 5 when it is built upon. A SUGGESTION from one team may be a BLOCKING issue for a less experienced student.

What Counts as a Well-Defined Step

Not everything on a page is a testable step. The following table clarifies what you are testing versus what you are not:

You are testing You are NOT testing
Whether every link in the nav goes to the right page Whether the academic content is scientifically correct
Whether every referenced file or tool is accessible and functional Whether you agree with the deliverable design
Whether a student could follow the stated instructions without help Whether the page looks visually polished (unless it prevents reading)
Whether code examples run without errors Whether there are alternative ways to accomplish the task
Whether referenced external tools (GitHub, ATLAS, Colab) actually exist and work Whether you would personally structure the assignment differently

Week 8 Deliverable

Due: By end of Thursday class. Push the following to your audit branch:

  • docs/phase3_audit/section_X/walkthrough_log.md — at least 10 observations, each with page, step, what-happened, severity, suggested fix, and files-affected fields
  • docs/phase3_audit/section_X/README.md — a one-paragraph summary of your section: what it is supposed to do, what state it is in, and the three most critical issues you found

Grading (10 pts): 6 pts for completeness and specificity of walkthrough log; 2 pts for severity classifications that are clearly justified; 2 pts for README summary.
