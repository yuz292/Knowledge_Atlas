About the Tracks

COGS160: AI & VR is organized around four complementary research tracks that together build and validate the ATLAS system — a web-based platform for organizing and analyzing evidence from environmental psychology. Each track involves 3 pairs of students (6 students per track) working on distinct but interconnected challenges over Weeks 3–7.

The tracks are: Image Tagger (scientific tagging of architectural interiors), Article Finder (evaluating the evidence pipeline), VR Stimulus (designing validated experimental stimuli), and GUI Evaluation (improving the interface for all user types).

Each track has its own learning objectives, team assignments, and deliverable timeline. Click on any track below to view the full planning document.

Week 1: Shared Foundation — D0 due Mon night Week 2 (All Tracks)

Manual Literature Search (D0): All students across all four tracks spend Week 1 on a systematic manual literature search for evidence on environmental psychology topics. D0 (Query Diary + 500-word reflection) is due Monday night of Week 2 — before track-specific work begins. This one-week sprint establishes the baseline evidence corpus and calibrates everyone's understanding of what counts as high-quality evidence.

By the D0 deadline, all students will understand: the evidence landscape in environmental psychology, the ATLAS system's role in organizing that evidence, and the quality standards their track will apply for the rest of the semester.

Image Tagger
Scientific Tagging & Validation
1
Build and validate the CNFA scientific image tagging system. Implement deterministic and VLM-based extractors for 424 environmental psychology constructs (e.g., biophilic richness, spatial openness). Create spatial bounding, tag validation tests, and connect image libraries to experimental stimuli.

Key Deliverables (Weeks 3–7)

  • D1 (Week 3, 10%): Triage Table of Feasible Tags
  • D2 (Week 5, 15%): Known-Case Tests & Math Implementations
  • D3 (Week 7, 20%): Interim Validation Report

VR Stimulus Design
Experimental Environment Validation
3
Design and validate VR scenes for environmental psychology experiments. Map visual properties to psychological constructs, implement spatial configurations, validate perceptual realism and construct validity, and create reproducible scene specifications. Ensure VR stimuli align with ATLAS evidence.

Key Deliverables (Weeks 3–7)

  • D1 (Week 3, 12%): Visual Property Map
  • D2 (Week 5, 13%): Scene Specification & Realism Test
  • D3 (Week 7, 20%): Validation Report

GUI Evaluation
Interface & Usability
4
Systematically evaluate ATLAS's interface for usability across all user types (researchers, students, architects, domain experts, admins). Conduct user testing with personas, identify pain points, prototype AI help systems, propose GUI improvements, and prepare the system for Fall 2026 COGS160: AI & VR use.

Key Deliverables (Weeks 3–7)

  • D1 (Week 3, 12%): Refined User Profiles
  • D2 (Week 4, 12%): AI Capability Assessment
  • D3 (Week 5, 15%): Simulated Expert Panel Critique
  • D4 (Week 6, 11%): Personalization Proposal
  • D5 (Week 7, 20%): Full Usability Report

Phase 3 · Weeks 8–10 · All Tracks

Site Audit & Hand-Off

Teams audit a section of this site they did not build, fix what they can, write formal success conditions, and produce a hand-off report for the Fall instructor. Work is conducted in the Tag-UCSD/Designing_Experiments repository.

Each team is assigned one of the four site sections (Image, Article Finder, VR, GUI) they did not build. The cross-assignment ensures genuine naivety in the student walkthrough. Work is submitted as a Pull Request to the upstream repo.
