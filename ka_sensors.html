Page purpose (for workflow and interface designers): This is a reference and orientation page. Its audience is students in COGS 160 [Experimental Design in Human Factors] and researchers who are selecting or comparing measuring instruments for a study. It explains what each signal / measuring instrument measures, which cognitive or affective constructs it is primary or auxiliary evidence for, what setup effort and artifact risks are involved, and where to find technical guidance. It is not an article search page. Students come here when they are in the Operationalization stage of their hypothesis and need to decide which instruments to use; researchers come here to understand the warrant tier of a signal for a given construct. Article search — finding published studies that use a given instrument or construct — is a distinct workflow reachable via the button above.
