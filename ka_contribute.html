Help Build the Atlas

Every paper you contribute makes the whole system smarter

The Knowledge Atlas relates scientific articles to one another — linking theories, constructs, and neural mechanisms across studies. The more articles we have on any given topic, the more precisely we can identify contradictions, trace warrant chains, assess replication strength, and surface the questions that matter most. Your contribution, whether a single PDF or a carefully chosen set of papers, directly strengthens the epistemic infrastructure that researchers and students depend on.

Why Every Paper Counts

Each article the Atlas processes produces dozens of typed propositions — claims about constructs, theories, and mechanisms that are automatically linked to every other proposition in the system. A single paper on attention restoration, for example, might yield evidence that strengthens three warrant chains, introduces a novel measurement instrument, and surfaces a contradiction with stress recovery theory that nobody had noticed before.

The Atlas is not a bibliography. It is an epistemic engine — and you are one of its sources of fuel.
