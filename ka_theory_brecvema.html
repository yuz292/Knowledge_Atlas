At a glance

In plain English. BRECVEMA (Juslin, 2013) is the most comprehensive contemporary account of the mechanisms by which music, and by extension other aesthetic stimuli, induce emotion. The eight mechanisms are: Brain stem reflex (loud sudden sounds), Rhythmic entrainment (synchronisation of motor and autonomic rhythms), Evaluative conditioning (learned pairings with affective events), Contagion (mimicry of perceived emotional expression), Visual imagery (triggered scenes and bodily simulation), Episodic memory (autobiographical association), Musical expectancy (prediction violation and resolution), and Aesthetic judgment (deliberate evaluation). The model maps onto four T1 frameworks: Predictive Processing (PP) for expectancy; Interoception (IC) and Neuromodulation (NM) for brain-stem and autonomic routes; Embodied Cognition (EC) for entrainment and contagion. Panel-review pending on which mechanisms generalise beyond music to architectural, landscape, and visual-art aesthetics — the Juslin group has begun this extension (Juslin, Harmat, & Eerola, 2014) but the empirical programme is young.
