Complicated surfaces index

The harder pages of Knowledge Atlas

The Atlas has ordinary pages — a home, a topic browser, an article view — that most visitors can navigate without guidance. It also has a second class of pages that demand more of both the designer and the reader: epistemic and Bayesian network viewers, an interpretation layer, an argumentation layer, a question-answering surface, mechanism and theory deep-dives, gap and evidence-landscape maps, research-front trackers, and two structural inspectors. These pages exist because the underlying representation demands them — you cannot adequately show a web of belief in a simple list — but each one poses a design problem that ordinary page patterns do not solve. This index lists them and their current implementation status.

Every linked page on this index follows the same scaffold: what it displays, who needs it, why the design is hard, the current naive solution, and a critique scaffold for each user type. If you are a Track 4 student, these are candidate T4.a / T4.c targets; if you are a researcher, they are where the site's interpretive claims live.

Article Finder problems · AF-1 to AF-3

Article Finder — specs for new functionality

Three T2-track build-out specs: harvesting candidate articles from the references of corpus papers; ROI-based candidate generation driven by EN and argumentation analysis; deepening the neural-underpinnings (PNU) layer. Each is a feature that does not yet exist; the spec pages describe the problem, the challenges, what a real implementation would call, and invite per-user-type critique — same six-section scaffold as the other journeys, amber-coded to flag them as build-out rather than as critique-of-existing.

  • AF-1 · Articles referred to in corpus absent
  • AF-2 · ROI-based candidate generator absent
  • AF-3 · Plausible Neural Underpinnings absent

ℹ︎Reading the status labelswhat the badges mean

naive — the page exists but shows the simplest possible implementation; it is a placeholder that serves its function badly. Good T4 critique target.
prototype — a first serious attempt; the structure is right but affordances are incomplete. Good redesign target for T4.f.
shipped — the page has been through at least one round of user testing and is considered production-quality for its scope.
absent — the page does not yet exist; the link here is a spec document, and no implementation has been attempted. These are the highest-leverage Track 4 redesign targets because the critique step has to imagine what the page should do before any design can be proposed.
