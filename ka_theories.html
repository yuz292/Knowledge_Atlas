Theories

Knowledge Atlas organises theories of environmental cognition in two tiers. Tier 1 (T1) frameworks are the foundational, mostly domain-neutral mechanisms that underpin how people perceive and respond to their surroundings. Tier 1.5 (T1.5) domain theories apply two or more T1 frameworks to a specific architectural or environmental concern. Tier definitions and mappings follow THEORY_HIERARCHY_IMPLEMENTATION.md.

Tier 1 — Foundational frameworks

Eleven mostly domain-neutral mechanisms: ten cognitive / neural systems and one superordinate configurer that decides when each system is recruited.

PPPredictive Processing

Perception, attention, and affect are driven by prediction, prediction error, and precision-weighting; environmental structure shapes what the agent predicts and how confidently. In-depth entry →

SNSalience Networks

Environmental cues recruit orienting, task-switching, and threat / surprise-detection systems, producing rapid reorientation to novelty-relevant or safety-relevant features. In-depth entry →

DPDefault-mode & Place

Place encoding, internally directed cognition, and spatial memory binding mediate environmental experience through hippocampal and default-mode circuitry. In-depth entry →

DTDual-Task / Attentional Load

Environments influence cognitive load, attentional depletion, and the competition between concurrent task demands and restoration. In-depth entry →

NMNeuromodulatory Systems

Environmental stimulation modulates arousal, reward, vigilance, and restoration through dopaminergic, serotonergic, cholinergic, and related neuromodulatory systems. In-depth entry →

ICInteroceptive-Constructionist Affect

Environmental conditions alter bodily state and affective interpretation through interoceptive signalling and insula-centred appraisal; affect is constructed, not read off a stimulus. In-depth entry →

MSMultisensory Integration

Coherent or incoherent cross-modal signals shape perception, comfort, and material interpretation through superior colliculus and cortical multisensory regions. In-depth entry →

ECEmbodied Cognition

Bodily state, posture, action simulation, and perceived affordance participate in how spaces are perceived, categorised, and valued; meaning is not primarily symbolic. In-depth entry →

CBChronobiological Regulation

Light and temporal structure regulate circadian entrainment, daytime alertness, and physiological timing through retinohypothalamic and suprachiasmatic pathways. In-depth entry →

MSIMotor-Sensory Integration

Motor planning and sensory feedback are coupled in a closed loop that supports navigation, manipulation, and spatial prediction during movement through an environment. In-depth entry →

IE_DPTImplicit-Explicit Dual-Process Theory

A superordinate configurer that decides, for any situation, whether implicit / automatic or explicit / controlled processing is recruited — meta-configuring which other T1 systems operate. In-depth entry →

Tier 1.5 — Domain application theories

Thirteen mid-level theories, each mapping to two-to-four T1 parents. These are the theories most frequently named in the applied literature; each is a specific composition of foundational mechanisms.

ARTAttention Restoration Theory

Parents: PP · SN · DP  ·  Kaplan & Kaplan · 1989

Natural environments passively engage involuntary attention and allow directed attention to replenish, producing measurable recovery on executive-function tasks after exposure.
In-depth entry →

SRTStress Reduction Theory

Parents: IC · NM · PP  ·  Ulrich · 1983

Exposure to natural settings produces a rapid, affect-mediated decrement in autonomic arousal, independent of cognitive appraisal.
In-depth entry →

BIOPHILIABiophilia Hypothesis

Parents: NM · IC · EC  ·  Wilson · 1984; Kellert et al.

Humans carry an evolved affinity for natural life and life-like forms; exposure to nature or biomorphic design elicits embodied safety signals and positive appraisal.
In-depth entry →

PROSPECT_REFUGEProspect-Refuge Theory

Parents: PP · SN · DP  ·  Appleton · 1975

Environments that afford both broad outward view (prospect) and sheltered vantage (refuge) are preferred because they satisfy evolved vigilance-and-safety heuristics.
In-depth entry →

PRIVACY_REGULATIONPrivacy Regulation Theory

Parents: IE_DPT · EC · IC  ·  Altman · 1975

Privacy is a dialectical, dynamic regulation of access to the self — people explicitly and bodily adjust interaction with others to achieve a desired level of contact at each moment.
In-depth entry →

KAPLAN_PREFERENCEKaplan Preference Matrix

Parents: PP · SN · IE_DPT  ·  Kaplan & Kaplan · 1982

Environmental preference arises from a four-factor balance of coherence, complexity, legibility, and mystery, reflecting the agent's need to understand and to explore.
In-depth entry →

ADAPTIVE_THERMALAdaptive Thermal Comfort

Parents: IC · NM · IE_DPT  ·  de Dear & Brager · 1998

Thermal comfort is adaptive rather than absolute: occupants' preferred temperature tracks recent outdoor conditions via homeostatic signalling, autonomic regulation, and behavioural adjustment.
In-depth entry →

SPACE_SYNTAXSpace Syntax

Parents: PP · EC · IE_DPT  ·  Hillier & Hanson · 1984

Spatial configuration — not individual rooms — drives movement, encounter, and understanding of a building or urban area, through visibility, embodied navigation, and habit / wayfinding.
In-depth entry →

SOUNDSCAPESoundscape Theory

Parents: PP · SN · NM · IE_DPT  ·  Schafer · 1977; ISO 12913

The acoustic environment as perceived in context; its interpretation depends on predictive pattern recognition, saliency filtering, arousal regulation, and explicit listening strategies.
In-depth entry →

PLACE_ATTACHMENTPlace Attachment

Parents: IC · DP · IE_DPT  ·  Low & Altman · 1992

An affective bond between person and place arising from repeated emotionally meaningful experience, place memory, and the integration of place into personal identity.
In-depth entry →

BRECVEMABRECVEMA — Music & aesthetic emotion

Parents: PP · NM · IC  ·  Juslin · 2013

Eight mechanisms of musically induced emotion: Brainstem reflex, Rhythmic entrainment, Evaluative conditioning, emotional Contagion, Visual imagery, Episodic memory, Musical expectancy, and Aesthetic judgment.
In-depth entry →

FLOW_THEORYFlow Theory

Parents: PP · DT · NM  ·  Csikszentmihalyi · 1975

Optimal experience arises when perceived challenge matches perceived skill; attention narrows, load becomes well-regulated, and engagement systems sustain absorbed activity.
In-depth entry →

GOLDILOCKS_PRINCIPLEGoldilocks Principle

Parents: PP · NM · IE_DPT

Stimulation is valued when it is neither too much nor too little: inverted-U arousal, autonomic balance, and threshold regulation produce a preferred middle band for complexity, novelty, and density.
In-depth entry →
